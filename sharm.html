Welcome To South Sinai

South Sinai is a beautiful region in Egypt where the desert meets the Red Sea, creating stunning natural scenery. It is famous for its peaceful beaches, mountains, and tourist cities like Sharm El Sheikh, Dahab, Nuweiba, and Taba.

Top Attractions

Ras Mohammed National Park

Located south of Sharm El Sheikh at the tip of the Sinai Peninsula. It is famous for its exceptional coral reefs, rare marine species, and crystal-clear waters. What makes it unique is the meeting point of the Gulf of Suez and the Gulf of Aqaba, creating rich biodiversity and one of the best diving spots in the world. It is also a protected area that attracts scientists and nature lovers from around the globe

Nabq Protected Area

Located north of Sharm El Sheikh along the Gulf of Aqaba. It is known for its mangrove forests, desert landscapes, and diverse wildlife. The area offers a peaceful environment and is perfect for eco-tourism and nature exploration. It is also home to Bedouin communities that preserve traditional lifestyles.

Naama Bay

Located in the center of Sharm El Sheikh, Naama Bay is the main tourist hub of the city. It is famous for its lively atmosphere, sandy beach, and clear turquoise waters. The area is full of hotels, restaurants, cafes, and shops, making it ideal for both relaxation and entertainment. Visitors can enjoy swimming, diving, boat trips, and a vibrant nightlife, which makes it one of the most popular destinations in South Sinai.

Shark's Bay

Located near Sharm El Sheikh International Airport, Shark's Bay is a calm and beautiful coastal area known for its clear waters and rich coral reefs. It is less crowded than Naama Bay, making it ideal for relaxation, snorkeling, and diving. The bay offers stunning underwater scenery with colorful fish and marine life, along with luxury resorts and peaceful beaches. Its quiet atmosphere and natural beauty make it a perfect destination for visitors seeking comfort and privacy.

Blue Hole

Located north of Dahab along the Gulf of Aqaba. It is one of the most famous diving sites in the world due to its deep underwater sinkhole. What makes it unique is its depth, clear visibility, and reputation as both a beautiful and challenging diving location for professionals.

Blue Lagoon

Located about 10 km north of Dahab. It is known for its calm, shallow waters and soft sandy beaches. The peaceful atmosphere, windsurfing conditions, and natural desert backdrop make it perfect for camping and relaxation.

Colored Canyon

Located near Nuweiba, about an hour from Dahab. It is famous for its multi-colored rock formations shaped by natural erosion over thousands of years. It offers a unique desert adventure, hiking trails, and some of the best photography views in Sinai.

Tarabin Area

Located in northern Nuweiba. It is a Bedouin coastal area known for its simple camps and traditional lifestyle. It offers an authentic cultural experience with local food, music, and a very peaceful seaside atmosphere.

Ein Khudra Oasis

Located inland near Nuweiba in the desert. It is a green oasis surrounded by mountains, with palm trees and natural springs. What makes it unique is its contrast between desert silence and fresh water life, making it a hidden natural gem.

Saint Catherine

is a breathtaking mountain area in South Sinai, Egypt, known for its dramatic peaks, clear skies, and peaceful atmosphere. It is surrounded by rugged mountains and famous for its ancient monastery, rich history, and unique desert beauty, making it one of Egypt’s most remarkable natural and spiritual destinations.

Salah El-Din Castle

Located on Pharaoh's Island. It is a historic fortress built during the Crusades for defense. It combines rich medieval history with panoramic views of the Red Sea and surrounding mountains.

Taba Heights

Located south of Taba. It is a luxury resort area featuring high-end hotels, golf courses, and private beaches. What makes it special is the combination of mountains, sea, and modern tourism facilities, offering a very relaxing and upscale experience.
