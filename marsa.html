Welcome to Marsa Alam

Marsa Alam is an Egyptian tourist city in the Red Sea Governorate. It is considered one of the most beautiful diving areas in Egypt and the world, and a favorite destination for safari enthusiasts. It boasts a unique collection of islands, including Zabargad Island, Khawar Island, Rocky Island, and Shihab Al-Fuston. The city also features a range of unique mountains and valleys, as well as a number of ancient mines that were excavated in the surrounding mountains during ancient times.

Top Attractions

Port Ghalib

Port Ghalib is an ideal tourist town located in Marsa Alam on Egypt's Red Sea coast, boasting private yacht marinas, sandy beaches, and five-star resorts. It's a great base for diving, shopping, and dining, and conveniently located just 4 km from Marsa Alam International Airport

The shrine of Abu al-Hasan al-Shadhili

The shrine of the Sufi saint Abu al-Hasan al-Shadhili is located in the desert village of Humaythra, south of Marsa Alam in the Red Sea Governorate of Egypt. The shrine and its adjacent mosque, built on an area of ​​7,000 square meters, are a major pilgrimage site for followers of the Shadhili Sufi order from around the world, especially during the annual Mawlid celebrations.

Sharm El Luli Beach

Sharm El Luli beach in Marsa Alam is considered one of the most beautiful beaches in the world, boasting crystal-clear turquoise waters and soft white sand, and is located within the Wadi El Gemal Protected Area. Renowned as a paradise for diving and snorkeling due to its rare coral reefs, it is an ideal destination for those seeking tranquility and ecotourism.

Hamata Island

The Hamata region lies south of Marsa Alam in the Red Sea Governorate and is an integral part of the Wadi El Gemal Protected Area. Here, the mountainous terrain blends seamlessly with the turquoise sea, creating a unique natural landscape. Its name derives from the sorrel plant, which evolved into "Hamata," and the region boasts rich biodiversity and breathtaking scenery.

Wadi El Gemal Reserve

Wadi El Gemal National reserve is located within the geographical boundaries of Marsa Alam city, south of the Red Sea Governorate. It is considered one of Egypt's most beautiful nature reserves and is divided into several sectors, including Qulaan, Hamata, and the land sector. The reserve is characterized by its rocky terrain in the western part, while it becomes sandy as it approaches the coast, which boasts long beaches suitable for swimming.

Al Nayzak Lake

Al Nayzak Lake in Marsa Alam is one of Egypt's most remarkable natural wonders. This stunning natural pool is located on the Red Sea coast, 8-14 km south of Marsa Alam city. Famous for its crystal-clear turquoise waters, it has a circular shape resembling a meteorite crater, believed to be the site of an ancient meteorite impact. It is a safe spot for swimming and snorkeling, away from strong currents.
