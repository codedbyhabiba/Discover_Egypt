Welcome To Suez

Suez is a timeless city where the sea meets the world, standing at the gateway of the Suez Canal. It carries a powerful spirit of movement and connection, where history and global journeys pass through one remarkable place.

Top Attractions

Ain Sokhna

Located on the western coast of the Red Sea, about 120 km from Cairo. It is one of the most important coastal tourist areas near the capital, known for its natural hot springs that give it its name (“Ain Sokhna” means Hot Spring). It features modern resorts, water sports facilities, and is a popular quick escape for Cairo residents due to its short distance and accessibility.

Port Tawfik

Located at the southern entrance of the Suez Canal near the city of Suez. It is a quiet coastal area known for its harbor views and peaceful atmosphere. Visitors enjoy watching ships passing through the canal and walking along the waterfront.

Suez Port

Located at the southern end of the Suez Canal within the city of Suez, Suez Port is one of the most important strategic ports in the world. It plays a vital role in connecting global trade routes between East and West, What makes it special is its constant sense of movement and life, as it serves as a meeting pointfor the world in one powerful location.

Suez Corniche

Located along the eastern edge of the city of Suez. It is a well-known public promenade that reflects the local lifestyle of the city. What makes it unique is its role as a social gathering place for residents, especially in the evenings, and its view of passing ships near the canal entrance, combining daily life with maritime activity.

Mount Ataka

Located northwest of the city of Suez, Mount Ataka overlooks the Gulf of Suez in a breathtaking panoramic view. It is part of the Eastern Desert mountain range, What makes it truly special is its high elevation, offering a stunning view over the Suez Canal region, making it an ideal destination for adventure lovers and those who enjoy exploring the quiet beauty of the desert.
