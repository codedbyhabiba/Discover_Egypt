Welcome to Siwa Oasis

Siwa Oasis is one of the most important and beautiful desert tourist destinations in Egypt, located in the Western Desert near the Libyan border. It is famous for its unique natural landscape, which combines sand dunes, natural springs, and stunning salt lakes. The oasis is also well known for its large number of palm trees and olive groves, making it one of the main producers of dates and olives in Egypt. Siwa is characterized by its peaceful and quiet atmosphere, far from the noise of big cities, which makes it an ideal place for relaxation and meditation. In addition, it has a unique Bedouin culture and its own local language that reflect a rich history and identity. It also includes many historical and archaeological sites that attract tourists from all over the world.

Top Attractions

Salt Lakes

The salt lakes are one of the main attractions in Siwa. Their high salt concentration allows people to float effortlessly. They contain natural minerals that are beneficial for the skin and relaxation. Their clear blue color creates a breathtaking natural view.

Shali Village

Shali Village is the ancient and historical town in Siwa Oasis. It was built using a material called “kershef,” a mixture of mud and salt, which makes its architecture unique. Large parts of it were damaged due to heavy rains, but it still preserves its traditional and historical spirit. Today, it is an important tourist attraction for history lovers and photography enthusiasts.

Dakrour Mountain

Dakrour Mountain is one of the most important tourist and therapeutic landmarks in Siwa. Visitors come from different places for sand therapy, as the hot sand helps relieve joint pain and rheumatism. An annual festival is held there, combining the cultural and traditional heritage of the oasis residents. It is also known for its high location, which offers a stunning panoramic view of the surrounding desert.

Bedouin Camps

Bedouin camps in Siwa are considered one of the most important tourist experiences that reflect the traditional lifestyle of the oasis people. They are simple places located in the heart of the desert, built in a traditional Bedouin style using wood, mud, or tents. Visitors enjoy calm sitting areas under the stars, along with drinking Bedouin tea and tasting local food. They also include evening gatherings around the fire with folk music and traditional stories that reflect Siwa’s culture.

Cleopatra Spring

Cleopatra Spring is one of the most famous natural springs in Siwa. It is known for its clear, continuously flowing water, making it suitable for swimming all year round. It is said that Queen Cleopatra visited it and bathed there, which is why it was named after her. It is considered one of the most important tourist attractions where visitors go to enjoy and relax.
